* Outline
A missing boy returns to his family after an unexplained absence. The play explores the impact of his disappearance and return on the family, particularly on Kevin and his sister Nessa.

* Characters
|------+------------------------------------------------------|
| Kevin | 11yo boy. The returner. Quiet, sensitive kid.       |
| Nessa | Kevin's sister, 13yo in Act I, 22yo in Act II.      |
| Cáit  | Mother. 40-50ish, struggling with circumstances.   |
| Ben   | Father. 40-50ish, means well but bumbling at times. |
|------+------------------------------------------------------|

* Act I
** Scene 1: Moving Day
*** Kitchen and hall. Afternoon. Moving day. Mid-afternoon light. Front door open. Boxes in hall and kitchen. The house not yet lived in.
*** Ben enters through the front door carrying a box and a rolled rug.
**** Ben
Right. Where are we putting everything.
*** Cáit in the kitchen, unwrapping plates.
**** Cáit
Not there.
**** Ben
I've only come in.
**** Cáit
Then don't stop there.
*** Ben drops the box just inside the door.
**** Ben
Temporary.
*** Kevin enters behind him dragging a duvet.
**** Kevin
Which one's mine.
**** Ben
The small one.
**** Kevin
That's not fair.
**** Ben
It's character building.
**** Kevin
I don't want character.
**** Ben
You'll thank me.
**** Kevin
I won't.
*** Nessa crosses the hall with a backpack, heading straight upstairs.
**** Cáit
Hold on now.
**** Nessa
I'm only looking.
**** Cáit
Looking with your bag.
**** Nessa
In case I find one.
**** Ben
Nobody's claiming rooms.
**** Nessa
Too late.
*** Nessa exits upstairs.
**** Kevin
I'm going.
**** Cáit
You're not.
**** Kevin
She'll take the best one.
**** Ben
There is no best one.
**** Kevin
There always is.
*** Ben considers this.
**** Ben
Fair.
**** Cáit gives him a look.
**** Ben
Go on so. But no fighting.
*** Kevin runs upstairs.
**** Cáit
Why do you let them.
**** Ben
It's a house, Cáit. Let them run in it.
**** Cáit
It's too big.
**** Ben
It's grand.
**** Cáit
It's too big.
**** Ben
We'll grow into it.
**** Cáit
We're not growing any more.
**** Ben
Speak for yourself.
*** Beat.
*** Cáit looks around the kitchen, taking it in.
*** The kettle begins to boil.
*** Lights shift                                                    :transition:


** Scene 2: The Attic
*** An attic. Pitched roof, gabled. A few dusty boxes and artefacts left by the previous occupants — shapes under sheets, a trunk, the dark bulk of a dress form. A small stained window high in the gable end lets in a weak, uneven light — enough to see by, not enough to make out detail. The floor is boarded. Downstage, a square hatch set into the floor allows a tiny peak of brighter light.
*** From below, through the hatch, muffled: sound of doors opening and closing. The kids hunting for bedrooms.
**** Kevin offstage
This one's bigger.
**** Nessa offstage
They're all the same.
**** Kevin offstage
That one has a better window.
**** Nessa offstage
It's the same window.
**** Kevin offstage
It's not.
**** Nessa offstage
You don't know what you're talking about.
**** Kevin offstage
I do.
*** A beat.
**** Kevin offstage
What is it?
**** Nessa offstage
Don't touch it.
**** Kevin
Why?
**** Nessa
Because.
*** Muffled effort. Then the thud of the ladder dropping.
*** A shaft of landing-light falls through the hatch into the attic, catching dust.
**** Kevin offstage
Jesus, that nearly hit me in the head!
**** Nessa offstage
Told you!
*** Beat.
**** Nessa offstage
No, I'll check it out. You stay put, god only knows what you'd do.
*** Nessa appears through the hatch, hands on the rim, pulling herself up. She stands in the light. Looks around.
**** Nessa
Jesus.
**** Kevin
What.
**** Nessa
It's mad up here.
**** Kevin
Let me up.
**** Nessa
I said wait.
**** Kevin
I don't want to wait.
**** Nessa
Wait.
*** She moves further in, her shadow crossing the light. A box label visible where a moment ago it wasn't.
**** Kevin
What's up there.
**** Nessa
Stuff.
**** Kevin
What stuff.
**** Nessa
Stuff.
*** Beat.
**** Nessa
Come up. Slowly.
*** Kevin climbs. His head appears, caught in the light. He looks around without moving the rest of him.
**** Kevin
Woah.
**** Nessa
I know.
*** He pulls himself up and stands. The two of them in the shaft now — everything else still half-dark.
**** Kevin
Who's stuff is it.
**** Nessa
Whose.
**** Kevin
Whose stuff is it.
**** Nessa
The people who lived here.
**** Kevin
Before us.
**** Nessa
Yeah.
**** Kevin
Why'd they leave it.
**** Nessa
I don't know.
**** Kevin
Maybe they died.
**** Nessa
Kevin.
**** Kevin
What.
**** Nessa
Don't.
*** He wanders further. Touches a box.
*** Carstairs                                                       :prop:
**** Nessa
What.
**** Kevin
It's written on the box. Carstairs.
**** Nessa
That was their name.
**** Kevin
Weird name.
**** Nessa
It's a name.
*** Kevin has reached a stack of comics. He crouches.
**** Kevin
Look.
**** Nessa
Leave them.
**** Kevin
They're class.
*** He sits, already opening one.
**** Kevin
Carstairs.
**** Nessa
What.
**** Kevin
The comic. It's called Carstairs as well.
**** Nessa
It's not.
**** Kevin
It is.
*** He shows her. She glances. Turns away.
**** Nessa
Weird.
*** Nessa moves about the space, restless. Opens a trunk. Closes it. Lifts a corner of a sheet draped over something. Drops it.
*** Kevin reads, half to himself.
**** Kevin
"Carstairs stepped into the—"
*** Nessa stops moving. Stands with her back to him.
**** Kevin
"—into the chamber and the—"
**** Nessa
Kevin.
*** He doesn't hear her.
**** Kevin
"—and the door shut behind him—"
*** Beat.
**** Nessa
Like a dog.
*** Kevin stops. He looks up.
**** Kevin
What.
*** Nessa does not turn.
**** Kevin
What did you say.
*** She still does not turn.
*** Kevin looks back at the comic. He does not read it.
*** Nessa waits a second longer than she should. Kevin does not look up.
*** Nessa goes to the hatch. Steps onto the ladder. Descends.
*** A beat.
*** The hatch swings shut. We are left with only the murky light from the stained glass window.
*** Kevin alone. The comic open on his lap. He looks toward the hatch, audibly sighs, and goes back to the comic, flipping through pages.
*** He closes it, goes over to the hatch, tries to open it.
**** Kevin shouts
Nessa!
*** A beat.
**** Kevin shouts again, impatiently
Nessa! Come on!
*** A beat.
*** Kevin returns to the comic, flips it open, starts reading again. The light through the window dims a little.
*** Kevin returns to the hatch, shakes it, it does not budge.
**** Kevin shouting louder
MAM! Nessa! COME ON!
*** He gives the hatch one more shake, the hatch opens, he descends down the ladder.
*** Lights fade                                                     :transition:


** Scene 3: Reunion
*** In the darkness the set changes. When the lights come up the house is the same house, but lived in. A table and chairs in the kitchen. Books on the shelves. A coat on the bannister. Nessa's schoolbag dropped at the foot of the stairs. A pile of post[fn:post] on the hall table. Dishes in the sink. Cáit's coats hang doubled on a single hook. A child's jacket — Kevin's — is folded carefully on a chair. On the hall table, a torch[fn:torch], spare batteries, and a clipboard thick with damp flyers. The top one a school photograph of Kevin.
*** The hall is empty. Evening light fading.
*** From upstairs, offstage:
**** Kevin offstage
Mam?
*** Nothing.
**** Kevin offstage
Nessa?
*** Beat.
**** Kevin offstage, sounding closer
Dad?
*** Silence. He starts back up.
**** Kevin calling as he goes
Nessa took the double room! How did she get all her stuff in already?
*** He disappears again into the upstairs.
**** Kevin offstage, farther away
Hello?
*** Sounds of Kevin walking around upstairs.
*** A key in the front door.
*** Cáit enters. Torch. Runners. Clipboard and flyers in hand. She shuts the door with the backward push of habit.
*** She places the clipboard on the hall table on top of the pile. We see that Kevin's face is prominent on the flyer.
*** She walks past the kitchen doorway. Stops. Turns back.
*** In the kitchen she takes a bottle of wine from a press[fn:press], opens it, pours fast into a glass, drinks half of it without sitting.
*** She exhales.
*** She removes her coat, comes back into the hall, reaches to hang it.
*** Looks up.
*** Kevin is halfway down the stairs. Comic in hand.
*** Cáit's hand falls loose at her side. The glass tips upside down but does not fall. Wine spills across the floor.
*** Cáit makes a sound that is not a word.
**** Kevin casually
Nessa's gone mad.
*** Beat.
**** Kevin impatiently
She's been hours.
*** Cáit drops to her knees before him.
*** Her hands to his face. His hair. His shoulders. Checking and touching and not believing touch.
**** Kevin
Mam.
**** Cáit
Jesus Christ.
**** Kevin
What.
**** Cáit
Jesus Christ.
*** She pulls him towards her.
**** Kevin
Mam, you're hurting me.
*** The front door opens hard.
*** Ben enters mid-call, mobile to ear.
**** Ben
I've been round the shelters again. Nothing, as if he'd turn up at one at this stage. The Guards don't even answer the phone to me any more.
*** He sees them. Stops. The phone lowers from his hand.
**** Ben
Cáit.
*** Cáit cannot answer.
**** Ben
Kevin?
**** Kevin
Everyone's acting thick[fn:thick].
*** Ben crosses to them slowly, then quickly, then is there.
*** He kneels. Takes Kevin by the arms.
**** Ben
Look at me.
**** Kevin
I am looking at you.
**** Ben
Look at me.
*** Kevin looks at Ben.
*** Ben pulls the boy into him.
*** On the landing above, Nessa stands in the half-dark. Barefoot. Motionless.
*** Kevin sees her.
**** Kevin
There she is.
*** He points.
**** Kevin
She locked me up there.
*** Nobody hears him.
*** Cáit is crying openly now.
**** Ben
Ring someone. Jesus. Ring everyone.
**** Cáit
He's freezing.
**** Ben
Get a blanket.
**** Cáit pleading
He's here.
**** Kevin
I wasn't gone /that/ long.
**** Ben
It's alright. It's alright.
**** Kevin
I kept shouting.
*** Nessa grips the bannister but does not descend.
**** Kevin flatly
Nessa.
*** She does not answer.
**** Kevin to Nessa, more urgently
Why didn't you come back.
*** Nobody hears that either.
*** The family closes round him.
*** Lights fade                                                     :transition:

[fn:post] Post is a term used in Ireland to refer to mail or letters.
[fn:thick] In this context, "acting thick" means acting stupid or not understanding something.
[fn:torch] A torch is a flashlight.
